Neuroimaging Method

Latest MRI & brain methods across top journals.

  1. Image: Article page preview

    Cholinergic basal forebrain neurons regulate vascular–glymphatic coupling in aging

    BOLD–CSF coupling and cholinergic function indicate aging-related clearance impairments; basal forebrain alterations relate to neurovascular–glymphatic coupling.

    Why it matters: Connects neurovascular regulation and glymphatic clearance in aging.

    Reviewer’s skepticism: Human analyses are largely correlational; causal links remain tentative.

    Chuang, K. H., et al. (2025). Nature Communications. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-60812-3

Lifespan Development & Aging

Structure, function, neurovascular/glymphatic aging in humans.

  1. Image: Article page preview

    Age-related constraints on the spatial geometry of the brain

    Geometric deformation fields from structural MRI reveal age-related patterns of expansion and compression across cortical axes with cognitive correlates.

    Why it matters: Introduces geometric deformation as an aging marker beyond thickness/volume.

    Reviewer’s skepticism: Cross‑protocol stability and biological interpretability remain open.

    Escalante, Y. Y., et al. (2025). Nature Communications. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-025-63628-3

Cognitive Reserve

CR/BR/resilience frameworks with MRI central, including MRI→PET prediction.

  1. Image: Article page preview

    Classifying mild cognitive impairment from normal cognition: fMRI complexity matches tau PET

    Functional MRI complexity measures differentiate MCI from controls at levels comparable to tau PET, implying functional integrity proxies pathology.

    Why it matters: Supports MRI as a PET surrogate for resilience modeling.

    Reviewer’s skepticism: Cross‑sectional design; predictive value over time untested.

    Jann, K., et al. (2025). Alzheimer’s & Dementia. https://doi.org/10.1002/dad2.70159

Imaging Biomarker in the AD Continuum

Preclinical → MCI → AD; multimodal MRI; MRI–PET links; progression risk.

  1. Image: Article page preview

    A radiomics model predicts progression from mild cognitive impairment to Alzheimer’s disease using structural MRI

    Structural MRI radiomics from ADNI predicts MCI→AD conversion with validation AUC≈0.88, combining imaging with cognitive indices.

    Why it matters: Demonstrates practical MRI‑only progression prediction.

    Reviewer’s skepticism: External multi‑center generalization remains to be proven.

    Li, Y., et al. (2025). Scientific Reports. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-19586-3

Geriatric Mental Illness

Late-life depression, anxiety, psychosis, bipolar — MRI-centered evidence.

  1. Image: Article page preview

    Inflammatory and MRI perfusion biomarkers predict depression outcome

    Perfusion MRI and peripheral inflammation at baseline relate to 6‑month outcomes in depression, highlighting vmPFC/OFC/MFG perfusion patterns.

    Why it matters: Connects perfusion MRI to prognosis in late‑life depression.

    Reviewer’s skepticism: Generalizability to strictly geriatric cohorts remains to be tested.

    Xiong, W., et al. (2025). Translational Psychiatry. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41398-025-03587-x
